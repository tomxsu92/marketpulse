**Figma vs Linear: A Comprehensive Comparison**

In the realm of design and product development, collaboration tools have become an essential part of the workflow. Two popular options that have gained significant attention are Figma and Linear. While both tools serve the purpose of design collaboration, they have distinct features and functionalities that set them apart. In this comparison, we'll delve into the world of Figma vs Linear, exploring their features, pricing, and usability to help you make an informed decision.

**2. Feature Comparison**

### Design Collaboration

Figma excels in design collaboration, offering advanced features such as real-time commenting, @mentions, and file sharing. Designers can work together seamlessly, and stakeholders can provide feedback directly within the design file.

Linear, on the other hand, focuses more on product development and task management. While it does offer some design capabilities, its primary focus is on product roadmap planning, task assignment, and progress tracking.

### UI/UX Design

Figma is a robust UI/UX design tool, offering a wide range of features such as vector graphics, typography, and layout management. Designers can create high-fidelity designs and prototypes without leaving the platform.

Linear has limited design capabilities and is not a suitable replacement for dedicated design tools like Figma.

### Prototyping

Both Figma and Linear offer prototyping capabilities, allowing designers to create interactive prototypes that can be tested and shared with stakeholders.

### Task Management

Linear excels in task management, offering features such as task assignment, due dates, and progress tracking. This makes it an ideal tool for product development teams.

Figma, on the other hand, does not offer native task management features. However, its integration with other tools like Asana and Trello makes it easy to manage tasks and projects.

### Team Collaboration

Both Figma and Linear offer real-time team collaboration features, allowing multiple users to work together on designs and tasks.

### File Sharing

Both tools offer file sharing capabilities, making it easy to share designs and tasks with team members and stakeholders.

**3. Pricing Face-Off**

Figma offers a tiered pricing plan, with the following options:

* **Free**: Limited to 3 projects, 2 editors, and 30 days of version history.
* **Professional**: $12/user/month (billed annually), 3 projects, 5 editors, and 30 days of version history.
* **Organization**: $45/user/month (billed annually), unlimited projects, 5 editors, and 30 days of version history.

Linear offers a pricing plan with the following options:

* **Free**: Limited to 3 users, 1 project, and basic features.
* **Starter**: $9/user/month (billed annually), 5 users, 1 project, and basic features.
* **Pro**: $29/user/month (billed annually), 15 users, 5 projects, and advanced features.

**4. Who Should Choose Each**

### Figma

* Design teams and agencies who need advanced design collaboration and prototyping features.
* Companies who want to integrate design with other tools like Asana and Trello.
* Individuals who want to create high-fidelity designs and prototypes without leaving the platform.

### Linear

* Product development teams who need task management and product roadmap planning features.
* Companies who want to track progress and assign tasks to team members.
* Individuals who want to create product roadmaps and track progress without leaving the platform.

**5. Final Recommendation**

Figma and Linear are both excellent tools that cater to different needs. If you're a design team or agency looking for advanced design collaboration and prototyping features, Figma is the better choice. However, if you're a product development team or individual who needs task management and product roadmap planning features, Linear is the way to go.

In conclusion, while both tools have their strengths and weaknesses, Figma and Linear are both excellent options for collaboration and productivity. By understanding their features, pricing, and usability, you can make an informed decision that suits your needs.
